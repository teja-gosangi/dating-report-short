Trend 01 · The Pattern

Comfort is the new luxury.

The best dates weren't impressive. They were restful. People said Yes when they felt like they could stop performing and just be.

In a world where you're performing everywhere — at work, on socials, for family — a date that feels easy is the rarest thing.
MEANT2BAE
Trend 02 · The Pattern

Curiosity is the new attractiveness.

Being interested beats being impressive. The single biggest driver of a second date isn't how you look — it's whether you seem genuinely curious about the person across from you.

4×
"Easy to talk to" was mentioned 4× more than "attractive" in Yes responses.

Trend 03 · The City

Bangalore has a networking problem.

The most common complaint in our data: dates that felt like interviews. Urban professionals are so trained to pitch, impress, and exchange credentials that they default to it even on dates.

The city that's great at making connections is struggling to make romantic ones.

Trend 04 · The Mindset

Maybe is a good thing.

22% said Maybe — and that's not indecision, it's honesty. In a swipe culture that demands instant verdicts, these people are slowing down.

Saying "I need more time" is more rational than most decisions apps train us to make.

Why They Said No

What kills the room.

It's almost never about compatibility — profiling catches most of that. What kills a date is how people show up in the room.

01
The one-sided conversation
Making the date a monologue and not reciprocating.
02
The invisible wall
Polite, competent, emotionally distant. Felt like networking, not a date.
03
Missing effort & presence
Phone out. Late without owning it. Small acts that kill the room fast.
04
The ex factor
Talking about an ex — unprompted, at length, negatively.

The Asymmetry

Same date. Two realities.

How she remembers it
  • He didn't ask about me
  • It felt like networking
  • He was on his phone
  • No emotional warmth
  • Talked about his ex
How he remembers it
  • No spark or chemistry
  • Didn't feel attraction
  • Conversations were boring
  • She wasn't speaking much
  • Friendly, not romantic
She knows the exact moment. He just knows the feeling. Both are real.

The Takeaways

What 200+ first dates taught us.

01
Ask follow-ups.
Ask real questions, not interview ones. Listen and build on it.
02
Stop performing. Be there.
The best dates weren't the most impressive. They were the most comfortable.
03
Small things are big things.
Punctuality. Grooming. Knowing their name. Being kind to the waiter.
